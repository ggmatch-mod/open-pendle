# LENS: protocol-correctness

VERDICT: Sound under the protocol-correctness lens. Every load-bearing claim in the plan traces to the research digest, and the digest's own corrections and caveats are faithfully carried over rather than glossed: addresses and factory pairings match (all 5 market-factory/YCF pairs, router, commonDeploy, syFactory, redeemer, oracle, treasury); all five custom-error selectors match live captures; the PT↔YT direct-swap removal (Dec 2024 selector remap) and two-step multicall composition are correctly stated; fee mechanics are right including the per-(router,market) override gotcha, readState(actual-router) rule, 3% vs 5% YT fee by factory generation, and the ln(1.05) cap; expiry handling matches source-verified behavior (burn always works, redeemPyTo* without YT, exitPostExp* zero-impact path, pyIndexCurrent max()-guard with depeg note); the ExpiredLpPtRedeemer is correctly scoped to the 7 drained-YT whitelisted markets and correctly NOT claimed to fix the 9 hard-stuck markets; the M6 pre-flight checklist mirrors the verified revert conditions including the cross-generation parallel-PT silo and the MarketFactoryMarketExists front-run recovery; localStorage is display-cache-only with on-chain re-verification, keeping the trust boundary on-chain; and the fee-flow-to-Pendle requirement is correctly satisfied by construction. The referenced fork-test suites exist in research/fork-tests/ as claimed. The four findings are precision fixes (one wrong initData encoding for the 4th SY template, one over-attributed confidence label on RouterStatic ApproxParams generation, two under-specified trust/sequencing boundaries) — none change what gets built or the milestone order, and two would be caught by the milestones' own fork-test done-criteria.

## [minor] M7 — SY adapter creation (advanced path bullet)
PROBLEM: The plan asserts a single initData encoding for 'the 4 upgradeable/adapter templates': `initData = initialize(name, symbol, adapter)`. The research (and the checked-in fork test research/fork-tests/SYFactoryFork.t.sol, lines 425-434/503) verified that only the 3 WithAdapter templates use initialize(string,string,address); PendleERC4626NoRedeemNoDepositUpgSY uses initialize(string,string) and the 3-arg form reverts. The plan also omits that this 4th template has no dedicated commonDeploy wrapper — its combined SY+market deploy must go through deployCommonMarketById(bytes32 id, ...).
SUGGESTION: Split the bullet: 3 WithAdapter ids use initData = initialize(name, symbol, adapter); PendleERC4626NoRedeemNoDepositUpgSY uses initialize(name, symbol) and its one-tx market flow routes via deployCommonMarketById. M7's fork-test done-criterion would catch this at build time, but fix the plan text so the wizard isn't designed around a wrong encoding.

## [minor] §2 fact F6 and §3.2/M3 quote pipeline
PROBLEM: F6 claims RouterStatic 'generates ready-to-use ApproxParams (swapExact*StaticAndGenerateApproxParams)' with confidence 'Fork-test executed'. The research verified only the two PT-direction generators (swapExactSyForPtStaticAndGenerateApproxParams, swapExactTokenForPtStaticAndGenerateApproxParams) and only by signature inspection, not execution; no YT-direction or add-liquidity generator was verified to exist. What was fork-executed is the default createDefaultApproxParams path (auto-delegation to ActionSimple facets). M3's quote pipeline as written assumes generated ApproxParams for all four swap directions.
SUGGESTION: Relabel F6's generator claim as 'verified signatures, PT directions only' and make the fork-verified default ApproxParams the primary M3 path, with RouterStatic-generated params as a gas optimization to be confirmed per-function (enumerate the deployed RouterStatic facet selectors in M0) before the pipeline depends on them.

## [minor] §3.4 Security posture / M1 paste-validation
PROBLEM: Calling the isValidMarket OR-check 'the security gate' overstates what it proves. It validates only that the market shell is canonical factory bytecode; per F1, createYieldContract accepts ANY contract claiming IStandardizedYield, so a fully 'valid' market can wrap a malicious SY that receives user deposits via router zaps/mints (getTokensIn is attacker-controlled). The plan's 'trade at your own risk' framing partially covers this, but the trust boundary is never stated explicitly, and a maintainer could reasonably treat post-gate markets as safe for approval UI.
SUGGESTION: State the boundary explicitly in §3.4 and M1: isValidMarket is necessary but not sufficient — the SY (and its tokensIn list) under any community market remains untrusted code; approvals and deposit simulations must treat the SY as adversarial even after the market validates, and the pool page should surface SY provenance (deployed via Pendle's syFactory templates vs unknown bytecode) as a distinct trust signal.

## [minor] §3.2 Quoting / §3.4 / M2 / M6 (simulate-before-sign)
PROBLEM: The plan gates 'Confirm' on a final eth_call simulation of the exact router/helper call from the user's address, with exact-amount approvals as the default. Until the approve tx is mined, that simulation reverts on allowance (the research explicitly notes eth_call execution 'needs balances/approvals or state overrides'), so the gating logic as specified will misreport failures for every first-time trade and for M6's helper pre-flight (commonDeploy requires seed-token approval first). The plan never says how this is resolved.
SUGGESTION: Specify the mechanism: either sequence the pipeline as approve → wait for confirmation → simulate → enable Confirm, or use eth_call state overrides on the token's allowance/balance slots (supported by Arbitrum Nitro nodes, but verify on the default public RPC since users can supply their own). This is a design decision that belongs in §3.2, not an implementation surprise.

# LENS: scope-sequencing

VERDICT: Sound plan under the sequencing lens — the macro order (load/trade before create, M0 foundations, M6 before M7) is correct and every claimed dependency checks out against the research, including the checked-in fork suites M0 relies on. Two changes are warranted before execution: pull the basic isExpired() gating out of M5 into M1/M2 (138 of the 146 loadable markets are expired, so M2–M4 otherwise ship revert-prone trade UI for the typical pasted address), and replace M1's unverifiable '146 markets render correctly' gate with a scripted enumeration-and-sweep harness (research validated only 9 sampled markets). Remaining findings are polish: bound M6's event-scan recovery so it works on the default public RPC, assign the two orphaned risk-table features (oracle-init CTA, out-of-range banner) to milestones, fix the stale 'requirement #8' label, and flag the aggregator-zap scope cut for explicit user sign-off since the requirement text asks for parity with the official interface.

## [major] M2–M5 (milestone cut of expired-market handling)
PROBLEM: The isExpired() gating lives entirely in M5, but per the plan's own research 138 of 146 loadable Arbitrum markets are expired and only 8 are active. From M2 through M4 the app will render actionable trade/mint/add-liquidity UI on the majority of markets a user can paste, where every action reverts with MarketExpired (0xb2094b59). Simulation-before-sign catches it pre-signature, but the staged app presents dead-end UI for the typical pasted address for three milestones, and M2/M3/M4 'Done when' demos never exercise expired input — meaning the expired code path gets bolted on late instead of being a foundation of the pool-page state machine.
SUGGESTION: Re-cut: move the basic market state machine (isExpired() check, disable swap/mint/add-liquidity/zap tabs, a placeholder 'Matured — redeem support coming' banner, and the registry 'Matured' badge) into M1/M2. Keep M5 as the milestone that adds the actual redeem/exit flows (redeemPyToToken, exitPostExpTo*, ExpiredLpPtRedeemer routing, depeg note). Add 'pasting an expired market shows disabled trade UI, not revert-prone controls' to the M2–M4 Done-when criteria.

## [major] M1 'Done when'
PROBLEM: 'Pasting any of the 146 existing Arbitrum markets (minus the 9 broken ones) renders a correct overview' is not verifiable as written. The research verified readState on only 9 sampled markets (first+last per vintage) and explicitly warns 'per-market SY-specific breakage beyond the 9 identified broken markets may exist' (e.g. assetInfo assetType=2 bridged tokens, odd decimals, exotic SY reverts). Manually checking ~137 markets is impractical, 'correct' is undefined, and the 146 count drifts as new pools deploy. As stated, the gate will either be rubber-stamped after spot-checking a handful of markets (false confidence in the flagship paste-any-address feature) or balloon into unbounded QA.
SUGGESTION: Replace with a scripted acceptance harness: enumerate all markets from the 5 factories' CreateNewMarket events (handling the v1 4-arg event shape the research flagged), run the M1 reader over all of them in a multicall sweep asserting load-without-error and sane invariants (expiry parses, tokens resolve, TVL ≥ 0), plus manual correctness spot-checks of ~10 named markets against known values. Phrase the criterion dynamically ('every market enumerable from the 5 factories at test time') rather than pinning 146.

## [minor] M6 (MarketFactoryMarketExists recovery) vs §3.2 data-layer rules
PROBLEM: M6's recovery path 'resolve the existing market via CreateNewMarket event scan' depends on eth_getLogs, which the research itself could not run against public Arbitrum RPCs ('rejected large ranges') — and the plan's default is a public RPC with no indexer. Built naively as a factory-history scan, this task fails on the plan's own default infrastructure.
SUGGESTION: Specify in the M6 task that the scan is bounded: an exact-tuple collision requires the colliding market to have been created at (nearly) the same block.timestamp, so scanning the last few hundred blocks with topic2=PT suffices; alternatively precompute the market's CREATE2 address (constant salt bytes32(chainid), per research) and skip logs entirely. Same note applies to any factory-wide market enumeration used elsewhere (M1 acceptance sweep should use a paid/archive RPC in CI, not the app's default).

## [minor] §6 Risks table → milestones
PROBLEM: Two user-visible features exist only as risk mitigations with no owning milestone task: (1) the optional 'initialize oracle' transaction (cardinality bump for fresh pools) and (2) the out-of-range pool banner (the risks table assigns wizard education to M6 but the pool-page banner to nothing). Orphaned scope like this silently drops or lands ad-hoc.
SUGGESTION: Assign both: out-of-range detection/banner belongs on the pool page (M1 overview or M3 trade panel, where the 0.96-proportion pre-check already lives); the 'initialize oracle' CTA belongs in M6's success flow or M8 — or explicitly move both to §7 non-goals if cut.

## [minor] §3.3 Pool registry heading
PROBLEM: The registry is labeled 'requirement #8' but the user's requirement list has 7 items (load-by-address + remember/forget is #3). §4's macro-feature numbering (1–5) also differs from the user's 7-item numbering, so the dangling '#8' looks like a stale edit and can mislead a maintainer cross-referencing requirements against milestones.
SUGGESTION: Fix the label to reference the user's requirement #3 (or the plan's macro feature #3) and add a one-line note in §4 explaining the mapping between the user's 7 requirements and the plan's 5 macro features (fees #7 = satisfied by construction, Arbitrum-only #6 = global constraint).

## [minor] §4 mapping table / M4 zap scope
PROBLEM: The table maps macro feature 4 ('Full integrations … zap/unzap') to M2+M3+M4 without qualification, but M4 restricts zaps to tokens the SY natively accepts and defers aggregator zaps to v1.5 (§7). The user's requirement text explicitly says 'like the official Pendle interface', whose zap is aggregator-powered from arbitrary tokens — so the mapping table overstates v1 delivery of that requirement.

# LENS: product-ux

VERDICT: Under the product/requirements lens the plan is sound and unusually well-grounded. All of the user's requirements map cleanly to milestones: pool creation (M6), one-click SY adapters (M7), paste-to-load with exact remember/forget checkbox semantics — per-pool tick saves to versioned localStorage, persists across visits, untick forgets, multiple pools each with individual state (M1, §3.3), full trade/mint/wrap/liquidity/zap integrations (M2–M4), maturity handling including the redeemer-whitelisted and hard-stuck legacy markets (M5), Arbitrum-only scoping, and fees-to-Pendle satisfied by construction and fork-verified (F3). The milestone ordering rationale (trade before create, since creation lands users on a pool page) is coherent, the wizards are honest about immutable rate bands, seeder-gets-YT, syOwner trust, and upgradeability without being unusable, and the plan's factual claims check out against the research digest. No blockers or majors: the six minor findings are journey gaps (first-visit empty state deferred to M8 in a paste-only-discovery app, near-miss paste errors, wallet/network states, deploy-receipt recovery, registry export) plus one disclosed scope cut — v1 zaps limited to SY-accepted tokens rather than 'like the official Pendle interface' — that should get explicit user sign-off rather than ride in as a non-goal.

## [minor] §3.2 Data layer rules / M4 Liquidity & zaps / §7 Non-goals
PROBLEM: Requirement #4 says zap/unzap 'like the official Pendle interface', which zaps from arbitrary tokens via aggregators. The plan restricts v1 zaps to tokens the SY itself accepts (getTokensIn/Out, SwapType.NONE) and defers aggregator zaps (KyberSwap/ODOS) to v1.5. This is disclosed and does not violate the backend-free principle (aggregator calls are client-side third-party APIs), but it is the one place the plan consciously undershoots a stated requirement, and the plan proceeds as if the cut is already approved.
SUGGESTION: Get explicit user sign-off on the v1 zap scope before M4, or pull client-side aggregator zaps into M4 as an optional stretch task. For most community pools the SY's tokensIn is the underlying/vault the user already holds, so the cut is likely acceptable — but it should be the user's call, not the plan's.

## [minor] M1 / M8 (first-visit empty state)
PROBLEM: Market discovery is paste-only by design and 'empty/error/loading states everywhere' is deferred to M8, the final milestone. A first-time visitor with no PLP address in hand has literally nothing to do — there is no pool list, no sample, and no guidance on where PLP addresses come from. This is the weakest journey for the non-technical user the wizards otherwise cater to, and it stays unspecified through M1–M7 staging demos.
SUGGESTION: Define the first-visit home state in M1: paste box front-and-center, a one-paragraph 'where to find a PLP address' explainer (Arbiscan, project announcements, MarketDeployment events), and optionally a small static starter list of the 8 currently-active V6 community markets, clearly labeled as examples/unvetted.

## [minor] M1 (paste-an-address flow)
PROBLEM: Non-technical users will paste PT, YT, or SY addresses instead of the market (PLP) address. The plan only specifies 'reject invalid with a clear warning' — a generic rejection strands exactly the users requirement #3 targets, since isValidMarket correctly returns false for a pasted PT.
SUGGESTION: Add a near-miss classifier to the paste flow: probe the rejected address for PT/YT/SY interface signatures (e.g. SY(), YT(), IStandardizedYield methods) and show a specific message like 'This looks like a PT token — paste the market (PLP) address instead', ideally resolving the market when cheaply possible (e.g. via the paired factory's mappings or a bounded event scan).

## [minor] M0 / milestones generally (wallet & network journeys)
PROBLEM: Wrong-network handling (wallet on mainnet), wallet-less read-only browsing, and disconnected-state behavior are not tasks or acceptance criteria anywhere; M0's done criterion is only 'wallet connects on Arbitrum'. RainbowKit covers most of the switch-chain UX, but read-only viewing without a wallet is a product decision that affects every page and should be decided up front, not discovered at M8.
SUGGESTION: Add to M0/M1 acceptance criteria: (a) wrong-network state shows a switch-to-Arbitrum prompt and disables tx buttons; (b) all pool overview/registry features work with no wallet connected (reads are RPC-only anyway); (c) positions/trade panels degrade gracefully to 'connect wallet'.

## [minor] M6 / M7 (deploy success recovery)
PROBLEM: If the deploy tx confirms but the app misses the receipt (closed tab, RPC drop, wallet-only broadcast), the new market address is never auto-saved — and because this app has no market directory, a lost address is unusually costly: the creator cannot rediscover their own pool inside OpenPendle. The failed-deploy path (error decoding, MarketFactoryMarketExists recovery) is well covered; the succeeded-but-unobserved path is not.
SUGGESTION: Add a small 'recover my deployment' affordance: scan commonDeploy MarketDeployment events filtered by the connected deployer address from a checkpoint block stored at wizard start (bounded range, works on public RPCs), and/or accept a pasted tx hash to extract the market address from the receipt.

## [minor] §3.3 Pool registry
PROBLEM: Requirement #3's 'persists across visits' rests entirely on localStorage, which is origin-scoped and clearable: the planned IPFS mirror (different gateway = different origin), browser data clearing, or a device switch silently loses the whole saved-pool list with no recovery path.
SUGGESTION: Add export/import of the registry (JSON download and/or shareable URL encoding the pool addresses) to M1 or M8. Cheap to build, keeps the backend-free promise, and makes the persistence semantics robust for the IPFS-hosting future the plan itself anticipates.

# LENS: skeptic

VERDICT: Sound plan — unusually well-grounded for this stage. The three assumptions I was asked to attack largely survive: (a) the zero-custom-contract claim is backed by executed fork tests of every macro flow (router accepts never-whitelisted markets, commonDeploy/syFactory work permissionlessly end-to-end, post-expiry exits verified per vintage), and fee flow to Pendle is contract-enforced, so requirement #7 is indeed satisfied by construction; (c) the localStorage registry design is defensible (versioned, re-validated, never trusted for tx building). Nothing is infeasible from a static site — no CORS blockers, and my event-scan attack on the duplicate-market flow mostly refuted itself (timestamp-derived tuples make a bounded recent scan sufficient). The two findings that survive with teeth are both under-verified links the plan presents as verified: the RouterStatic quoting leg (never executed, unknown fee-override context — could force an architecture change in M3's quoter toward simulation-first) and M7's token screening (specified with fork-test capabilities a browser doesn't have, leaving a silent-under-collateralization path for FOT/rebasing tokens). Both are fixable with targeted fork-test assertions and mechanism specs before M3/M7 — neither invalidates the milestone order or the frontend-only scope. No blockers.

## [major] §2 F6 / §3.2 Quoting / M3
PROBLEM: F6 labels the RouterStatic quoting leg 'Fork-test executed', but the research only fork-executed the real router with createDefaultApproxParams; RouterStatic's swapExact*StaticAndGenerateApproxParams calls were signature-verified only, never executed against any market. Critically, the research proved fees are per-(router, market) with ~4-10x Router V4 discounts on listed markets (readState(0).lnFeeRateRoot = 5.98e15 vs readState(router) = 1.4e15 on the live USDai market) but never established which fee context RouterStatic uses internally. Concrete failure: on every Pendle-listed discounted market — exactly the markets users will compare against app.pendle.finance — RouterStatic may quote at the base fee, understating output, and the tight generated ApproxParams (guessMin/guessMax centered on the wrong netPtOut) can exclude the true execution value, causing 'approx fail'/range-overflow reverts in the mandatory pre-sign simulation. M3's quote display, price impact, and implied-APY-after-trade all inherit the error.
SUGGESTION: Add an M0/M3 fork-test assertion that RouterStatic quote ≈ actual router simulation output on (i) a live fee-discounted listed market and (ii) a fresh undiscounted community market, before building the trade panel around *StaticAndGenerateApproxParams. If they diverge, restructure the quoter to be simulation-first: eth_call the real router with createDefaultApproxParams (fork-proven floor) as the primary quote, using RouterStatic only for price-impact decomposition. Downgrade F6's confidence label so the maintainer doesn't treat this as settled.

## [major] M7 token screening
PROBLEM: M7's 'detect fee-on-transfer (simulated transfer delta)' has no concrete browser primitive: a static frontend cannot measure a transfer delta via plain eth_call (no state mutation across calls; injecting balances requires per-token storage-slot discovery via eth_call state overrides, which public RPCs support unevenly). The fork tests that 'verified' FOT blocking used deployed mock tokens — a capability the shipped app won't have. Concrete failure path the plan leaves open: user picks a FOT token (or an Aave aToken — rebasing tokens DO exist on Arbitrum) in the simple path, deploys SY-only via deploySY (no seeding, so nothing reverts), then wraps tokens — PendleERC20SY silently under-collateralizes (fork-verified behavior) and later redeemers' withdrawals revert; any pool built on it strands LPs. The helper-seeding simulation only catches FOT when seeding with the raw token in the same tx.
SUGGESTION: Specify the actual detection mechanism in the plan: (1) always run the full deploy+seed eth_call simulation and map its revert to 'likely FOT' — which argues for making SY-only deploys of PendleERC20SY require a token-screen pass or an explicit override; (2) implement balance-slot discovery via eth_call state-override probing with graceful degradation on RPCs that reject overrides; (3) ship a hardcoded rebasing/FOT denylist covering known Arbitrum classes (aTokens, etc.). Treat 'M7 done-when: FOT mocks blocked' as requiring the browser mechanism, not a forge re-run.

## [minor] M6 error handling (duplicate-market resolution)
PROBLEM: The 'resolve the existing market via CreateNewMarket event scan' step is unbounded as written: a full scan of the V6 factory spans ~88M+ Arbitrum blocks, and the research team itself had public-RPC eth_getLogs calls rejected for large ranges and dropped by rate limiting. From a browser on the default free RPC this flow would fail exactly when needed. However, the failure is self-limiting: tuple params derive from block.timestamp, so a MarketFactoryMarketExists collision almost always implies the conflicting market was created at the same timestamp — i.e., within the last few blocks.
SUGGESTION: Bound the scan to a small recent block window (last ~few minutes) as the primary resolution path, with 'retry next block' as the always-available fallback; only offer a deep scan on user-supplied archive-grade RPCs. Note this explicitly in M6 so the implementer doesn't build an unbounded getLogs call.

## [minor] M1 paste-validation / §3.2 address book
PROBLEM: isValidMarket OR-ed across a hardcoded 5-factory list will hard-reject legitimate markets created on any future factory generation. Pendle rotates generations roughly yearly (V5 retired Oct 2025), the research explicitly flags that the address list may grow, and the last rotation shipped a NEW helper address (poolDeployHelper → commonDeploy), so runtime resolution from commonDeploy's immutables does not cover a rotation that deploys a new helper. A stale static deploy would then tell users a valid new Pendle market is 'invalid' — actively misleading — until someone redeploys the site.
SUGGESTION: Keep the hard security gate, but split the rejection UX into 'fails validation against all known factories — either not a Pendle market or this app build is outdated (check for updates)', and have the M8 drift-check CI watch deployments/42161-core.json for new factory keys so rotation produces a PR, not a silent lockout.

## [minor] §3.2 Quoting / M2 tx lifecycle
PROBLEM: 'Final eth_call simulation of the exact router call from the user's address before enabling Confirm' silently assumes the token approval already exists — pre-approval, the exact-call simulation reverts on transferFrom, so with exact-amount approvals as default, every trade's simulation gate can only run after the approve tx is mined. The alternative (eth_call allowance state overrides) is inconsistently supported across public RPCs. Same ordering constraint applies to M6's pre-flight simulation of deploy5115MarketAndSeedLiquidity (needs seed-token approval to commonDeploy first). As written, the M2/M3 tx-lifecycle UX ('simulate → sign') is under-specified for the two-transaction reality.
SUGGESTION: Specify the lifecycle as approve → simulate → confirm, with quotes before approval coming from RouterStatic/default-params estimation clearly marked as indicative; attempt state-override simulation opportunistically and fall back gracefully on RPCs that reject overrides.

## [minor] §3.4 Security posture / §6 risk table
PROBLEM: The risk table frames 'factory validation gate' as the mitigation for 'users paste malicious market addresses → fund loss via approvals', but isValidMarket only proves provenance of the market contract — a canonical factory will happily have created a market over a malicious or honeypot SY (SY creation is permissionless, F1). Such a market passes every gate, the trade panel opens, and simulation can be defeated by an SY that behaves differently at execution (owner-flipped pause/adapter). Exact approvals cap the loss to the traded amount, and the disclosure framing exists, but the table as written could lead the maintainer to treat the gate as sufficient.
SUGGESTION: Amend the risk row to name the residual risk explicitly (valid market ≠ safe SY) and promote the SY trust signals already scattered in §3.4/M7 (owner address, proxy admin, paused state, upgradeability) into a required per-pool trust panel in M1, not just creation-wizard disclosures.
